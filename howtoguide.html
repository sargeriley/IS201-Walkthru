Residential Construction Guide

A step-by-step overview of the home building process

Building a house is a monumental project that involves coordinating various trades, materials, and regulations. Below is a high-level sequential breakdown of the construction phases.

  1. Site Preparation and Grading Before any building begins, the land must be cleared of trees and debris. The site is graded to ensure a flat surface and proper drainage.

    Read more: Earthworks (Engineering)
  2. Foundation Work The crew pours the concrete slab, basement, or crawl space walls. This is the structural base that supports the entire weight of the home.

    Read more: Foundation Types
  3. Rough Framing The skeleton of the house is erected. This includes the floor systems, walls, and roof trusses. The house takes its actual shape during this phase.

    Read more: Framing (Construction)
  4. Installation of Mechanicals Once the shell is up, the "guts" of the house are installed. This includes plumbing pipes, electrical wiring, and HVAC ductwork before the walls are closed up.
  5. Insulation and Drywall Insulation is placed in exterior walls and the attic to regulate temperature. Afterward, drywall (sheetrock) is hung, taped, and mudded to create finished walls.

    Read more: Drywall Construction
  6. Interior Finishes and Trim This phase involves installing interior doors, window sills, baseboards, and cabinets. Hardwood floors or tiles are usually laid down during this stage as well.
  7. Exterior Finishes While the inside is being finished, the exterior receives siding, brick, or stucco, along with final driveway paving and walkway construction.

    Read more: Siding Materials
  8. Final Walkthrough A final inspection is conducted to ensure all systems are working and building codes are met before the final occupancy permit is issued.
